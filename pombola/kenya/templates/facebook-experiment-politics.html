The National Assembly of Kenya

The National Assembly of Kenya is made up of 350 members.

The members of the National Assembly represent the people of constituencies and as well as special interests (youth, women, persons with disabilities and workers) in the National Assembly.

The roles of the members of the national assembly are to:

  • Represent the diversity of the nation;
  • Enact legislation;
  • Determine how public money is spent and;
  • Exercise oversight over the President, the Deputy President and other State officers.

Your Role In Democracy

Kenyan citizens are expected to monitor how politicians are performing to ensure high standards of leadership and integrity are respected and maintained. Politicians should:

  • Be elected based on personal integrity, competence and suitability in a free and fair election;
  • Be objective and impartial in decision making and in ensure decisions are not influenced by nepotism, favouritism and other improper practices;
  • Provide selfless service based on public interest;
  • Be accountable to the public for decisions and actions; and
  • Demonstrate discipline and commitment in service to the people.

As a Kenyan citizen, you have the opportunity to hold your representatives to account in the national elections scheduled to take place in August 2017.

Registering to Vote

In order to vote, you must be registered. To be eligible to register to vote you must be:

  • A Kenyan citizen
  • Aged 18 years and above
  • Have a national ID or a Passport

To register, you must go to your nearest registration centre.
